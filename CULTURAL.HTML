Religious Culture

Religion in India is characterised by a diversity of religious beliefs and practices. Throughout India's history, religion has been an important part of the country's culture and the Indian subcontinent is the birthplace of four of the world's major religions, namely, Hinduism, Buddhism, Jainism, and Sikhism, which are collectively known as native Indian Religions or Dharmic Religions

Festivals

India is also known for its many colorful festivals, including:

  • Diwali - the festival of lights
  • Holi - the festival of colors
  • Eid - the festival of breaking the fast
  • Christmas - the celebration of the birth of Jesus Christ
  • Vaisakhi - the harvest festival of Punjab

Clothing

Traditional Indian clothing varies by region and religion, but some of the most recognizable styles include:

  • Saree - a draped garment worn by women
  • Dhoti - a wrapped garment worn by men
  • Sherwani - a long coat-like garment worn by men for formal occasions
  • Kurta - a long shirt-like garment worn by men and women

Cuisine

Indian cuisine is known for its use of spices and flavors, and varies greatly by region. Some popular dishes include:

  • Butter chicken - a creamy chicken dish
  • Tandoori chicken - a marinated chicken dish cooked in a clay oven
  • Chana masala - a spicy chickpea dish
  • Biryani - a mixed rice dish
  • Idli and dosa - savory pancakes made from fermented rice and lentils
